# Total-exonerations-in-US-from-2015-2025
Dataset and methodology for US exonerations 2015-2025
## Overview: 
- This project analyzes 341 exoneration cases in the U.S. between 2015 and 2025 using data from the National Registry of Exonerations. 
- The purpose is to examine how exonerations are distributed across states, how they have changed over time, and especially the human cost for how many years exonerees lost before their release.
- The findings show that violent crimes are associated with significantly higher years lost compared to nonviolent crimes, and that a relatively small number of states accounted for a large share of cases.
- This matters because exonerations show how long it can take for mistakes in the criminal justice system to be corrected and represent years of life taken from individuals.
## Data Sources: 
- The primary data source is the National Registry of Exonerations.
- It includes all known exonerations in the U.S., filtered to include only cases where both the conviction year and exoneration year fell between 2015 and 2025.
- Additional variables were used and created to deepen analysis, including a column for classification of crime type (violent vs. nonviolent).
## Methodology: 
- The dataset was cleaned to include relevant variables like name, state, county, conviction year, exoneration year, crime category, crime type, sentence, years lost, and date of release.
- The analysis for the project will be focused on three major patterns: increase of exonerations over time, sum of years lost by state and exoneration year, differences in years lost between violent and nonviolent crimes.
## Limitations: 
- Does not show the wrongful conviction rate because it does not include the total number of convictions by state or year.
- It only includes cases where both conviction year and exoneration year occurred between 2015 and 2025.
- Excludes people convicted before 2015 but exonerated during the period analyzed.
- Only captures formally recognized exonerations, meaning it does not include undiscovered ones.
- The "years lost" reflects time spent incarcerated  and does not capture the emotional and financial harm it entails.
## Visuals: 
- The project will include visuals like pivot tables and charts to emphasize impact, not just counts. As already said, some of these visuals include: sum of years lost by exoneration year, sum of years lost by state, and a comparison of years lost for violent vs nonviolent crimes.
- These visuals help show trends over time and make the findings easier to understand.
