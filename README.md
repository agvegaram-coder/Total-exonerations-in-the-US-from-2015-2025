# Total-exonerations-in-the-US-from-2015-2025
Dataset and methodology for US exonerations 2015-2025
Overview: This project analyzes exoneration cases in the U.S. between 2015 and 2025 using data from the National Registry of Exonerations. The purpose is to examine how exonerations are distributed across states, how they have changed over time, and how many years exonerees lost before their release. The findings in this data show that a small number of states accounted for a large share of cases. This matters because exonerations show how long it can take for mistakes in the criminal justice system to be corrected and how that is distributed across states.
Data Sources: The primary data source is the National Registry of Exonerations. It includes all known exonerations in the U.S., filtered to include only cases where both the conviction year and exoneration year fell between 2015 and 2025.
Methodology: The dataset was cleaned to include relevant variables like name, state, county, conviction year, exoneration year, crime category, sentence, years lost, and date of release. The analysis for the project will be focused on three major patterns: exonerations by year, exonerations by state, and average years lost. 
Limitations: The data does not show the wrongful conviction rate because it does not include the total number of convictions by state or year. It also only includes cases where both conviction year and exoneration year occurred between 2015 and 2025. As a result, it excludes people convicted before 2015 but exonerated during the period analyzed. Also, the data only captures formally recognized exonerations, meaning it does not include undiscovered ones. Finally, the "years lost" reflects time spent incarcerated  and does not capture the emotional and financial harm it entails.
Visuals: the project will include three charts: exonerations by year, exonerations by state, and average years lost by exoneration year. These visuals help show trends over time and make the findings easier to understand.
